About the Program

The Social Development and Peace Building Program focuses on empowering youth, improving education access, and promoting literacy to build resilient, peaceful communities in the Rwenzori region.

Through targeted interventions in youth leadership, education support, and community literacy, we address the root causes of conflict while building sustainable development pathways.

Our approach combines direct service delivery with capacity building, ensuring communities have the skills and resources to drive their own development and peace processes.

Projects Under the Social Development Program

Our projects focus on youth empowerment, education improvement, and literacy enhancement to build peaceful, educated communities.

Peace Jam Project

Focus: Promoting peacebuilding, youth empowerment, and social responsibility.

Objectives:

  • To inspire young people to become active citizens and peacebuilders in their communities.
  • To build leadership and conflict resolution skills among youth through mentorship and dialogue.
  • To promote values of tolerance, empathy, and non-violence among students and community members.
Reached over 700 people

Education Project

Focus: Improving access, quality, and retention in education.

Objectives:

  • To increase access to quality education for children and youth, especially those in underserved areas.
  • To enhance the learning environment through provision of learning materials and school infrastructure support.
  • To reduce school dropout rates by promoting inclusive education and community involvement.
Over 38 direct beneficiaries and 105 indirect beneficiaries

Community Mobile Library

Focus: Improving literacy, reading culture and lifelong learning.

Objectives:

  • To promote a culture of reading and learning among children and adults in rural and hard-to-reach communities.
  • To improve literacy levels by providing access to books and learning materials where libraries are not available.
  • To support schools and teachers by complementing classroom learning with reading programs and mobile resources.
Over 350 direct beneficiaries and 500 indirect beneficiaries
