Health Promotion Program

Enhancing community well-being through public health awareness, preventive care, and sustainable health interventions in the Rwenzori region.

About the Program

The Health Promotion Program seeks to strengthen community health systems and promote preventive healthcare among vulnerable populations in Kasese and Ntoroko districts.

It focuses on addressing health challenges such as Hepatitis B, maternal health, and emergency response for disaster-affected households through innovative and sustainable interventions.

Through collaboration with local government, civil society, and health facilities, the program has reached thousands of community members, pregnant women, and children with lifesaving services.

Projects Under the Health Promotion Program

Our projects improve access to health services, promote disease prevention, and enhance community resilience.

Accelerating Hepatitis B Epidemic Control (AHepC-Rwenzori)

Goal: Strengthen Hepatitis B control among pregnant mothers in Kasese and Ntoroko districts.

Key Objectives:

  • Increase awareness and testing for Hepatitis B among pregnant mothers.
  • Improve enrollment of newly diagnosed mothers into care and treatment.
  • Enhance community, government, and civil society involvement in viral hepatitis service delivery.

Results: 50% of pregnant mothers reached and tested; 33% and 23% enrolled in care in Kasese and Ntoroko; improved multi-sectoral involvement. Reached 9,091 pregnant mothers directly and 2,112 indirect beneficiaries.

Tricycle Ambulance Project

Goal: Overcome transportation challenges in mountainous areas to ensure timely maternal healthcare.

Key Objectives:

  • Enable community-based emergency transport to healthcare facilities.
  • Reduce maternal and infant mortality in hard-to-reach sub-counties like Karambi, Nyakiyumbu, Bwera, and Ihandiro.
  • Collaborate with health facilities to ensure safe deliveries.
  • Prevent mother-to-child transmission of Hepatitis B, Syphilis, and HIV/AIDS through timely hospital access and Hepatitis B birth dose for newborns.

Sustainable Outcomes for Disaster-Affected Households

Goal: Support flood-affected families in Bulembia Division, Kasese Municipality with essential relief and sustainable livelihoods.

Key Activities:

  • Distributed food, clothing, and goats to affected households (180 direct, 200 indirect beneficiaries).
  • Trained 100 beneficiaries in Village Savings and Loan (VSL) group management, improving record-keeping and financial discipline.
