Enhancing community well-being by promoting public health awareness, preventive healthcare, and strong health systems in the Rwenzori region.

About This Program

The Health Promotion Program focuses on advancing public health and preventive services in the Kasese and Ntoroko districts of Uganda. Through awareness creation, early testing, and healthcare partnerships, the program strengthens community involvement in disease control and response.

Our flagship project — Accelerating Hepatitis B Epidemic Control in Kasese and Ntoroko Districts (AHepC-Rwenzori) — aims to expand access to testing, care, and treatment for pregnant mothers and their families.

This initiative empowers communities, government, and civil society organizations to jointly deliver sustainable viral hepatitis services, improving long-term public health outcomes in the Rwenzori region.

Project: Accelerating Hepatitis B Epidemic Control (AHepC-Rwenzori)

Empowering communities in Kasese and Ntoroko to reduce the burden of Hepatitis B through testing, treatment, and strengthened health systems.

Key Objectives

1) Increase the proportion of pregnant mothers who know their Hepatitis B status from 43% and 42% in Kasese and Ntoroko districts respectively.

2) Increase the proportion of newly diagnosed pregnant mothers enrolled in care and treatment from 23% and 10% in Kasese and Ntoroko.

3) Strengthen community, government, and civil society involvement in viral hepatitis service delivery in both districts.

Project Achievements

a) 50% of pregnant mothers were reached and now know their HBV status.

b) 33% and 23% of newly diagnosed pregnant mothers in Kasese and Ntoroko respectively were enrolled in care and treatment.

c) Improved community, government, and civil society participation in viral hepatitis service delivery.

Program Impact

This project directly reached 9,091 pregnant mothers as primary beneficiaries and indirectly impacted 2,112 individuals — including 2,000 babies and 100 health workers — across Kasese and Ntoroko districts.

These achievements highlight GLPC’s commitment to improving maternal and child health, disease prevention, and equitable access to quality healthcare in the Rwenzori region.
